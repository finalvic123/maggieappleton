<p>Being humans, we rarely learn things linearly.  
No one begins their JavaScript journey by reading <a href="https://www.ecma-international.org/ecma-262/5.1/">the TC39 language specification</a> line-by-line like a pedantic masochist.</p>

Instead we all cobble together a good-enough understanding, leaving holes here and there to fill in later.

<p>So far I've stumbled along without 100% grokking prototypical inheritance, or how `call, apply,` and `bind` work on functions.  
It's fine. Stuff mostly works. Until it doesn't   ¯\_(ツ)_/¯</p>

But at some point, it's useful to stop stumbling about and  peek back at those holes.
